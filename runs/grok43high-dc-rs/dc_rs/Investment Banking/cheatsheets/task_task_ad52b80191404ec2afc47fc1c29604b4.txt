## Solution Strategies for Recurring Task Shapes

<memory_item>
<description>
When an existing DCF model must be updated to midyear convention while preserving all other inputs and assumptions, modify only the timing exponents used in the present-value calculations; do not alter revenues, margins, growth rates, capex, working capital, tax rates, or terminal-value parameters.
</description>
<example>
Locate the discounting formulas; replace each year-n end-of-year exponent with (n - 0.5); recompute the PV of each cash-flow line and the total enterprise value; divide by shares outstanding to obtain per-share value.
</example>
</memory_item>

## Formulas, Definitions, and Conventions

<memory_item>
<description>
Midyear convention treats each period's free cash flow as occurring at the midpoint of the year. For annual periods the discount factor for year n therefore uses the fractional exponent (n - 0.5).
</description>
<example>
DF_n = 1 / (1 + r) ** (n - 0.5)
</example>
</memory_item>

<memory_item>
<description>
The final per-share DCF value is the enterprise value (sum of discounted FCFs plus discounted terminal value) minus net debt, divided by the number of shares, then rounded to the nearest cent.
</description>
<example>
Value_per_share = round( (EV - Net_Debt) / Shares , 2 )
</example>
</memory_item>

## Pitfalls, Edge Cases, and Verification Checks

<memory_item>
<description>
After applying midyear convention, confirm that only the discount exponents were changed and that the delivered numeric answer matches the required rounding and delivery format (nearest cent, no extra text).
</description>
<example>
Verify rounding: final numeric result rounded to exactly two decimal places; confirm no other model inputs were edited.
</example>
</memory_item>
