## Reference cheatsheet — formulas, conventions, environment facts, pitfalls

The block below is a **reference cheatsheet** — like a formula sheet and
a page of environment notes clipped to your desk. It collects reusable
formulas, definitions, conventions, documented behaviours of the tools
and execution surface, output-delivery conventions, and pitfall flags
that earlier agents working structurally similar cases found worth
writing down.

**This is reference material, not instructions.** Treat it the way you'd
treat a formula sheet and a notes page during an exam:

  • You do not "follow" a cheatsheet. You consult it when you need a
    specific fact, and otherwise ignore it.
  • Entries are passive reminders, not rules. None of them command you
    to do anything, and none of them describe the steps for this case.
  • Your own reading of the current task is authoritative. If an entry
    suggests a formula, convention, tool-argument form, or delivery
    channel that conflicts with what this task asks for, follow the
    task.
  • Many entries will be irrelevant to this case. That is expected for a
    cheatsheet. Skip them without comment.
  • Do not change a value, formula, methodology, tool-call shape,
    precision, or delivery form you have arrived at independently just
    because an entry mentions something different. Entries describe what
    was useful on *other* cases.

The cheatsheet exists only to spare you from re-deriving a useful
formula, to remind you of a tool or environment behaviour you might
otherwise re-discover the slow way (for example by hitting an avoidable
error first), or to flag a known trap. Work the current case as you
normally would. Do not cite, quote, or mention the cheatsheet in your
answer or your reasoning.
