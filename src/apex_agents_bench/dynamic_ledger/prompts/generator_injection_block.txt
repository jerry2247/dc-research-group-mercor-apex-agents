## Reference cheatsheet — formulas, conventions, tool quirks, pitfalls

The block below is a **reference cheatsheet** — like a formula sheet
clipped to your desk. It collects domain-specific formulas, conventions,
definitions, tool-environment facts, and pitfall flags that earlier
practitioners (or earlier instances of you) found worth jotting down.

**This is reference material, not instructions.** Treat it the way
you'd treat a formula sheet during an exam:

  • You do not "follow" a formula sheet. You consult it when you
    need a specific fact, and otherwise ignore it.
  • Entries here are passive reminders, not rules to apply. None
    of them are commanding you to do anything.
  • Your own analysis of the current case is authoritative. The
    cheatsheet never overrides it. If an entry suggests a formula
    or convention that conflicts with the task's own instructions,
    follow the task.
  • Many entries will be irrelevant to this case. That is expected
    for a cheatsheet. Skip those without comment.
  • Do not change a number, formula, methodology, tool-call shape,
    precision, or convention you've arrived at independently just
    because a cheatsheet entry mentions something different.
    Cheatsheet entries describe what was useful on *other* cases.

Solve the current case as you normally would. The cheatsheet exists
only to spare you from re-deriving a useful formula, to remind you of
a tool-environment fact you might re-discover the hard way, or to flag
a known pitfall you might otherwise step into. Do not cite or mention
the cheatsheet in your response.
